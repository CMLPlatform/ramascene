RaMa-Scene

RAw MAterials SCENario Efficiency improvement assessment tool and blended learning package.



Open tool

Project

RaMa-Scene is an EIT Raw Materials project with the objective of empowering and facilitating students, experts and decision makers in the analysis of the global economy and circular economy improvements. For this purpose, the project developed a blended learning educational package for lifelong learning and an easy to use web-application to create circular economy scenarios using Multi-regional Environmentally Extendend Input-Output Analysis.

Environmentally Extended Input-Output (EEIO)

EEIO is a linear economic and environmental accounting model which provides details on the relationships between economic actors (e.g. industries and final consumers) and the environmental pressures they exert as a result of their activities.

Data

The database behind the platform is EXIOBASE V3.3.sm (1995-2011) in Industry Technology Assumption (ITA) and product-by-product format. 200 products including secondary raw materials, 49 countries and 10 characterised impact indicators are available for selection to allow for complex multi-variate analysis.
Read more

Hotspot and Constribution analysis

Explore environmental and economic impact resulting from the production or consumption of goods and services. The *Hotspot* analysis allows for the investigation of impacts in the production of specific product categories while in the *Contribution* analysis impacts are assessed throughout the entire supply-chain of a consumed product category.
Read more

Main and comparison views

Compare historic data from EXIOBASE v3.3.sm using different analytical methods. Alternatively compare historic data with modelled counter-factual scenarios. This feature enables a comparative breakdown of dimensions via either sectoral (tree-map) or geographic (geo-map) visualizations.

Modelling

Create industrial or final onsumption scenarios by applying sets of relative changes to final demand or indutrial relationships. Multiple changes can be applied in sequence according to users' scenario modelling needs.
